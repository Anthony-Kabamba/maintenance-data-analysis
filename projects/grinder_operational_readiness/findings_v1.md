# Findings v1

## Project

Grinder Operational Readiness Inspection Framework (Prototype)

## Analysis Period

Synthetic inspection dataset consisting of 50 grinder inspection observations.

## Finding 1: Most Frequent Inspection Deficiencies

Housing vent condition and side-handle condition were the most frequently observed inspection deficiencies, each recording three failures during the observation period.

## Finding 2: Strongest Performing Grinder

AG-01 demonstrated the strongest operational readiness performance, recording twelve successful inspections and no inspection failures.

## Finding 3: Highest Failure-Rate Grinders

AG-03 and AG-05 exhibited the highest observed failure rates within the dataset and should be prioritized for further inspection review and defect investigation.

## Finding 4: Equipment Tag-Out Frequency

Seven inspections resulted in equipment tag-out, representing approximately 14% of all inspection observations. This indicates that a measurable proportion of grinder inspections identified conditions severe enough to justify equipment isolation from service.

## Limitations

The dataset is synthetic and intended for analytical and educational purposes only.

Inspection counts were not identical across grinders; therefore, failure-rate comparisons should be interpreted as preliminary indicators rather than definitive reliability conclusions.

Additional observations collected over a longer period would improve confidence in comparative asset performance assessments.
